Theory Reference

Chord Functions

Tonic chords provide stability and rest. Dominant chords create tension that wants to resolve. Subdominant chords provide motion away from tonic.

Roman Numeral Analysis

Roman numerals show the relationship of chords to the key center. Uppercase numerals indicate major chords, lowercase indicate minor chords.

Available Tensions

Extensions like 9th, 11th, and 13th can be added to chords to create more sophisticated harmonies. Some tensions may clash and should be avoided.

Scale Reference

Major Scale Modes

  • Ionian (Major): Bright, happy sound
  • Dorian: Minor with raised 6th
  • Phrygian: Minor with flat 2nd
  • Lydian: Major with raised 4th
  • Mixolydian: Major with flat 7th
  • Aeolian (Natural Minor): Dark, sad sound
  • Locrian: Diminished, unstable

Pentatonic Scales

  • Major Pentatonic: 1-2-3-5-6, great for melodies
  • Minor Pentatonic: 1-b3-4-5-b7, blues and rock
Common Progressions

Jazz Progressions

  • ii-V-I: The most common jazz progression
  • vi-ii-V-I: Extended ii-V-I with relative minor
  • I-vi-ii-V: Circle of fifths progression

Pop Progressions

  • I-V-vi-IV: Very popular in pop music
  • vi-IV-I-V: Alternative ordering
  • I-vi-IV-V: Classic 50s progression

Blues Progressions

  • 12-bar Blues: I-I-I-I-IV-IV-I-I-V-IV-I-V
  • 8-bar Blues: Shorter form
